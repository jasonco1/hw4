Tusks are actually...

An elephant's tusks are actually their incisor teeth! They generally start growing around two years old are used to aide in eating and a defensive tool.

Their tusks are made of ivory which unfortunately is a highly desired item. Sadly elephants are being hunted for their ivory faster than they reproduce. If you are interested in learning more, look in THE TRUTH page.

The World's Largest Land Mammal

At their full size an average male African elephant weighs up to 6 tons and as high as 3 meters tall!

What's the difference?

There are two species of elephants, African and Asian. They can told apart by the shape of their trunks and shape of their ears

An Asian elephant will have one "finger" at the tip of their trunk while an African elephants will have two.

Asians elephants have smaller ears that are shaped shaped like India. African Elephants have larger ears shaped like Africa.

1 Hour

Twenty minutes after being born elephants calves can stand and can start walking within an hour! Typically within two days a calve may keep pace with the herd. .
