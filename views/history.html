The Ivory Trade

In the 1970's the demand for ivory skyrocketed. Ivory was used to make billard balls, piano keys, jewlery, statues, etc; during this time. Elephants tusks are completely made of ivory, therefore were being targetedfor their tusks. During this period of time ivory was being sold legally while being taken illegally. Hundreads of thousands of elephants were being killed each year until more than half of the population was gone within the next decade.

Ivory was banned in 1989 by the Covention on International Trade in Endangered Species. Despite this, the legalization of stockpiling of remaining ivory to certain entities allowed certain markets to stay open and sell ivory legally. Because of this elephants poaching still continues as it may be sold illegally to these markets and camouflaged within the legal sale of ivory.Elephant poaching still remains the main threat to the elephant population today.

Refuse to Ride

Aside from the ivory trade, elephants still suffer for our enertainment. Baby elephants are poached form the wild and seperated from their families for tourist enertainment. They are beaten until they become obediant so tourists may ride them and so they may perform tricks. Refuse to ride elephants and only support responsible tourism projects.
